Activity 8 · Reading Checklist

  • ✓ The fire starts from a completely ordinary accident (tree branch)
  • ✓ The house "tries" to fight the fire with water and chemicals
  • ✓ The fire destroys Picassos and Matisses (priceless art)
  • ✓ One voice keeps reading poetry as everything else burns
  • ✓ The last standing wall still announces the date the next morning

Activity 10 · Intertextuality

The Poem Inside the Story

Bradbury chose Sara Teasdale's 1918 poem "There Will Come Soft Rains" as the title — and has the house read it aloud just before the fire begins. Read it carefully.

There will come soft rains and the smell of the ground,
And swallows circling with their shimmering sound;

And frogs in the pools singing at night,
And wild plum trees in tremulous white;

Robins will wear their feathery fire,
Whistling their whims on a low fence-wire;

And not one will know of the war, not one
Will care at last when it is done.

Not one would mind, neither bird nor tree,
If mankind perished utterly;

And Spring herself, when she woke at dawn
Would scarcely know that we were gone.
